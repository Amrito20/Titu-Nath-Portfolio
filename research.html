Hemodynamic Changes in Bifurcated Arteries with Varying Eccentricity Using Nanofluid Simulation Ongoing

January 2024 - Present
Undergraduate Thesis
Khulna University of Engineering & Technology

This research involves pioneering work to improve the hemodynamics of blood flow in bifurcated arteries using nanomedicine applications. The study focuses on computational fluid dynamics modeling to analyze how varying eccentricity in artery bifurcations affects blood flow patterns.

The research aims to address various cardiovascular issues by utilizing nanofluid properties in biomedical applications. The computational study focuses on understanding how different bifurcation geometries impact critical hemodynamic factors such as wall shear stress, recirculation zones, and potential for atherosclerotic plaque formation.

Thermoelectric Refrigeration: Analysis of Cooling Performance Using Peltier Effect Completed

November 2022 - December 2023
Course Project
Khulna University of Engineering & Technology

This research focused on the design, construction, and testing of a thermoelectric refrigerator utilizing the Peltier effect. The project explored alternative cooling technologies with potential environmental benefits compared to traditional vapor-compression refrigeration systems.

A working prototype was built to observe cooling effects under various operating conditions. The research analyzed the relationship between power input, temperature differential, and cooling efficiency. Results demonstrated the feasibility of thermoelectric cooling for small-scale applications while highlighting current efficiency limitations compared to conventional systems.
